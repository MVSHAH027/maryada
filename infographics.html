The Concept of Limits

Just like in cricket, life has a 'crease'. Stepping out recklessly leads to getting 'out'.

Life's Crease: The Limit

In cricket, a batsman is out if they leave the crease. Similarly, in life, a person who crosses their limits faces negative consequences. This 'crease' is our 'Maryada' - our boundary of respectful and appropriate conduct.

Limit Towards Elders

A fundamental limit is showing respect to our elders. We should never speak to them in a loud or disrespectful voice. Crossing this limit not only brings immediate negative results but also affects our future happiness. What we do to our parents, our children may do to us.

The Dress Limit (Dress-Crease)

Our traditional attire, like the Saree, embodies dignity, grace, and modesty. It represents our culture's soul. Adopting western dress without thought can disturb our inner balance and family life. Tight clothing can also cause physical health problems by affecting blood circulation and hormones.

The Behavior Limit (Vyavahar-Maryada)

Maintaining dignity and purity in public interactions is crucial. Shameless public behavior blurs the line between humans and animals. We must treat peers with respect, like siblings, and elders like parents, avoiding unnecessary contact to maintain this sanctity. Crossing this limit leads to societal decay.

Progress with Limits

True progress is not in abandoning limits, but in upholding them. A healthy, peaceful, and happy life is only possible within the framework of 'Maryada'. Every aspect of a civilized life has its own limit, which provides structure and ensures well-being.
